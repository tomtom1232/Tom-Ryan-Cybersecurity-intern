What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?
Agile is a more flexible approach to project management and focuses on collaboration and continuous improvement. 
It means delving into small but frequent updates. Agile adapts to sudden changes in the work environment evolving during the project. 
What are the core values and principles of Agile? (Hint: Check the Agile Manifesto.)
The core values and principles of Agile are valuing individuals and interaction over processes and tools, working software over comprehensive documentation. 
These values show flexibility, teamwork and delivery of value. 
What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?
Scrum utilises fixed-length development cycles called sprints, during which a team works on a set number of tasks within a specified timeframe. 
Scrum is often used when teams want structured planning and regular reviews, while Kanban is better for teams handling ongoing tasks or continuous work. 
Why does Focus Bear lean towards Kanban instead of Scrum?
I believe Focus Bear uses Kanban instead of Scrum because of its flexible task management. It will allow the team to work on tasks that arise rather than starting the next sprint.
