Roles in an Agile Team 🔍
Research & Learn What are the key roles in an Agile team, and what does each role do?
Developers are responsible for designing, building and keeping track of software or systems. 
They write code, fix bugs, implement new features and work closely with the rest of the team. 
QA (Quality Assurance) QA testers make sure the product works the way it should and meets the right standards. 
They must test features, identify bugs, and verify fixes for them, also ensuring the final product is ready for the public when released. 
Product Manager (PM), The product manager sees the direction of the product. 
They need to prioritise the features and manage the product backlog, gather requirements from stakeholders, and ensure the team is building something that supplies value to users. 
Agile Project Manager (Agile PM), The Agile project manager helps the team follow Agile practices. 
They remove obstacles, facilitate meetings like stand-ups, and ensure the team works efficiently. 
Other supporting roles (e.g., stakeholders, UX, customer support), 
Stakeholders provide input and feedback on the product, and UX designers focus on the user experience and interface design.
Lastly, the customer support helps the team provide feedback from users and helps see common and key issues. 

How do these roles collaborate in Kanban vs. Scrum? 
In Scrum, the team works in their own sprints with their own roles. Dailey Stand-ups and reviews are each a role of a sprint. 
In Kanban workflows, tasks move across a board with stages like To Do and In Progress. Roles collaborate more flexibly, focusing on improving workflow efficiency and limiting work progress.
