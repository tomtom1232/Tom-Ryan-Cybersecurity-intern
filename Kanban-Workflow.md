🔍 Research & Learn
How does a Kanban board work, and how does it help manage workflow?
A Kanban board is a visual tool that displays tasks as cards moving through columns. These represent the different stages of work. 
What do the different columns on a Kanban board represent? (e.g., Backlog, In Progress, Blocked, Done)
Each column represents a stage of the workflow, such as tasks waiting to start. This is a backlog. Tasks currently being worked on are in progress, and tasks being completed are done.
How do tasks move through the board, and who is responsible for updating them?
Tasks move across the board as work progresses from one stage to another. The person responsible for the tasks usually updates the progress. 
What are the benefits of limiting work in progress (WIP)?
Limiting WIP prevents team members from taking too many tasks on at once. This will help with efficiency, and it will also help the team finish tasks faster. 
📝 Reflection
How does Kanban help manage priorities and avoid overload?
Kanban makes priorities visible by showing what task needs to be completed next. This helps prevent overload and keeps the team focused on completing current tasks. 
How can you improve your workflow using Kanban principles?
I can improve my workflow by organising tasks and setting the status on each of them. Having a time limit on them could help as well to get them done.
