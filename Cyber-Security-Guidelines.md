What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?
Some very common cyber security threats in a remote work environment like Focus Bear are phishing attacks, unsecured home/public Wi-Fi and the use of unmanaged personal devices. 
Attackers can try to exploit these vulnerable to deploy malware and ransomware, also for financial gain. 

What are best practices for keeping your devices and accounts secure?
Some of the best practise to keep our devices and accounts safe are to enable multi-factor authentication on all of our accounts or using a password manager to create complex passwords for every site we use. 
Also having automatic updates set to on keeps operating systems to the latest and safest. 

Why is it important to lock your computer when away from your desk?
When locking your computer when leaving it is critical for safety. When locking your computer, it prevents unauthorized access to sensitive data and prevents any malicious software installation. 

How should you handle phishing attempts and suspicious links?
To handle finishing attempts and suspicious links, simply don’t click on them or open them or even reply. Report it to the email provider or scamwatch. 
Let the team know as well as soon as possible to prevent anyone clicking or open any suspicious emails. 

What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?
A strong password has to be 14-18 character this makes it harder to crack, with at least one symbol like @, #, and $. Also having an upper- and lower-case letter helps and numbers. 
A password manager could help with security alerts to help users see if their passwords has been reused or appeared in a data breach. 

Why is two-factor authentication (2FA) important, and when should you enable it?
Two-factor authentication is key for security, you should enable it because it adds another security layer beyond just your standard password protecting against phishing and unauthorised access even If your password was stolen.
