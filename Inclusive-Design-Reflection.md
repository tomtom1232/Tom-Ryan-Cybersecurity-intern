Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?
People considered vulnerable populations are people that have a disability or are older adults, children. People with disability may find it hard to navigate a inaccessible website with confusing interfaces for them, having difficulty understanding complex digital information. 

What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? (Hint: Avoid overwhelming UX, respect sensory needs, ensure clear communication.)
Ethical concerns include avoiding overwhelming designs having sensory sensitivities using clear and simple interaction and having digital tools inclusive and accessible for different cognitive needs. 

How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism? (e.g., simple language, predictable navigation, reducing cognitive load)
Interactions can be improved by using simple interfaces and language with clear instructions and minimal distraction and designs that can deuce the need of cognitive load. 

How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting? (Hint: Be clear in communication, respect different working styles, and allow flexibility.)
We can support neurodivergent members by communicating daily and clearly with them. Also respecting any different working environments, they have, providing flexibility and versatile structured tasks.
